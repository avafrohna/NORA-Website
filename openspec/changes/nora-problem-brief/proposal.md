## Why

Current healthcare environments are plagued by a documented "call bell burden," significant nursing time loss due to communication inefficiencies, and a critical language gap. NORA needs to establish its credibility and evidence-based approach by putting these problems front and center. A sparse, professional one-page site from a Harvard-award-winning team signals deliberate restraint and substance, capturing pilot interest and inbound inquiries.

## What Changes

- Develop a clean, one-page website for NORA with five key sections:
    - **The Problem**: Publicly documented clinical evidence (call bell burden, time loss, language gap).
    - **The Solution**: Outcome-focused value proposition: "Nurse arrives prepared. One visit. Any language."
    - **The Team**: Names, photos, and brief bios to establish credibility with investors and judges.
    - **Harvard Validation**: Highlighting "Australian winners of the Harvard University Health Systems Innovation Lab Global Hackathon."
    - **Contact/Call to Action**: Email/contact form for pilot interest: "Interested in piloting NORA in your facility — get in touch."
- Implement a "coming soon" / "in development" framing to set realistic expectations.
